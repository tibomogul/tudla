# Betting Table User Guide

The Betting Table is where stakeholders review shaped pitches and decide which ones to fund for the upcoming cycle. This follows the Shape Up methodology: a small group looks at a curated set of pitches and places bets on the ones worth building.

## Prerequisites

- You must be a **member** of the organization to view the betting table.
- You must be an **admin** of the organization to place bets or reject pitches.
- The cycle should be in the **Shaping** or **Betting** state for betting actions to be available.

## Accessing the Betting Table

1. Navigate to **Cycles** from the sidebar.
2. Click on the cycle you want to bet on.
3. Click the **Betting Table** tab at the top of the cycle detail page.

## Understanding the Layout

The betting table is divided into two areas:

### Pitch Cards (left)

Pitches are grouped by appetite:

- **Big Batch** (6 weeks) - larger projects shown with a purple badge.
- **Small Batch** (2 weeks) - smaller projects shown with a pink/secondary badge.

Each pitch card displays:

- **Title** - links to the full pitch detail page.
- **Appetite badge** - Big Batch or Small Batch.
- **Ingredients count** - e.g. "5/5 ingredients" indicates all sections are filled in.
- **State badge** - Ready for betting, Bet, or Rejected.
- **Author and date** - who created the pitch and when.
- **Problem excerpt** - a truncated preview of the problem statement.

### Team Availability (right sidebar)

Shows each team in the organization with:

- **Member count** - how many people are on the team.
- **Availability status** - "Available" (green) if the team has no projects committed to this cycle, or a warning showing how many projects are already committed.

## Betting Actions

When the cycle is in the Shaping or Betting state, each "Ready for betting" pitch card shows three actions:

### Preview

Click **Preview** to expand the pitch card and see all four ingredients inline:

1. **Problem** - what customer problem does this solve?
2. **Solution** - the shaped solution sketch.
3. **Rabbit Holes** - known risks and complexities to avoid.
4. **No-Gos** - what is explicitly out of scope.

Click Preview again to collapse. This lets you review pitches without leaving the page.

### Place Bet

1. Click **Place Bet** to open the bet confirmation dialog.
2. Select a **team** from the dropdown to assign the work to.
3. Click **Confirm Bet**.

This creates a new project from the pitch and assigns it to the chosen team for the cycle. The card updates immediately to show the "Bet" state with the assigned team name, and the team availability sidebar reflects the new commitment.

### Reject

1. Click **Reject** on a pitch you do not want to fund.
2. Confirm in the dialog that appears.

The pitch moves to "Rejected" state and the card greys out. Rejected pitches can later be moved back to Draft for rework from the pitch detail page.

## Read-Only Mode

When a cycle is in the **Active** or **Completed** state, the betting table displays a warning banner: "Betting is closed for this cycle." All pitch cards are visible for reference but no action buttons are shown.

## Tips

- Review the team availability sidebar before placing bets to avoid overcommitting a team.
- Use the Preview feature to compare pitches side-by-side without navigating away.
- Pitches that have already been bet or rejected appear greyed out so you can focus on the remaining decisions.
